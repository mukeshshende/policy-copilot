AI TOOLS ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
AIpportunity Pvt. Ltd.

1. PURPOSE

Artificial intelligence tools — including large language models, generative AI assistants, AI coding tools, image generation platforms, and AI-powered SaaS features — are increasingly embedded in professional workflows. AIpportunity Pvt. Ltd. actively encourages the use of AI tools to enhance productivity, accelerate delivery, and innovate. At the same time, AI tools introduce specific risks around data confidentiality, intellectual property, accuracy, and bias. This policy establishes the rules for responsible AI tool use. It applies to all employees, contractors, and interns at all locations.

2. APPROVED AI TOOLS

The following AI tools are approved for use with company data:

Tier 1 — Fully Approved (Enterprise-Grade, Data Protected):
- Microsoft Copilot for M365 (integrated in Teams, Outlook, Word, Excel) — company data processed within the Microsoft tenant boundary; no training on company data
- GitHub Copilot (for development roles with an active licence) — code suggestions; no code transmission for training under the enterprise agreement

Tier 2 — Conditionally Approved (Personal Accounts, No Company Data):
- ChatGPT (consumer tier) — permitted for general knowledge, ideation, and drafting of non-confidential content only
- Google Gemini — same conditions as ChatGPT
- Claude (claude.ai, consumer tier) — same conditions

Tier 3 — Not Approved:
- Any AI tool processing real client data, employee personal data, or data classified as Restricted or Confidential unless explicitly approved by IT Security
- Shadow AI: AI tools embedded in non-approved SaaS applications

If a tool is not on this list, employees must request approval from IT Security before use with company data.

3. DATA INPUT RESTRICTIONS

3.1 What Must Never Be Entered into Consumer-Tier AI Tools
The following categories of data must never be entered into consumer-tier AI tools (Tier 2) under any circumstances:
- Client names, project names, client data, or client code
- Employee personal data including names, contact details, performance data, or salary information
- Company financial data, forecasts, or pricing
- Source code or proprietary algorithms from client or company systems
- Contract terms, legal matters, or compliance documents
- Data classified as Internal (with identifiers), Restricted, or Confidential

The risk is that consumer AI tools may use inputs to train or improve their models, exposing confidential information permanently.

3.2 Acceptable Inputs for Consumer-Tier Tools
Consumer AI tools may be used for:
- Anonymised or hypothetical scenarios that do not reference real entities
- General knowledge questions and research
- Drafting templates, communication frameworks, or general documents with no confidential content
- Learning about AI capabilities, testing prompts with synthetic data

4. OUTPUT STANDARDS AND HUMAN OVERSIGHT

4.1 AI Outputs Are Not Authoritative
All AI-generated content must be reviewed and validated by a human before use in a business context. AI tools can hallucinate, generate inaccurate information, exhibit bias, and produce content that appears professional but is factually wrong. Employees are responsible for all content they submit — regardless of whether AI assisted in creating it.

4.2 Disclosure
Employees should be transparent about significant AI assistance in deliverables when submitting to clients unless the client has specifically indicated AI use is not permitted. Clients who have contractual restrictions on AI use must be identified by the Delivery team. Work for such clients must not use AI tools even for internal drafts.

4.3 Code Generated by AI
AI-generated code must be reviewed by the developer before commit. It must not bypass code review processes. Developers are accountable for understanding AI-generated code they submit. Blindly committing AI-generated code that introduces security vulnerabilities or bugs is treated as the developer's error, not the tool's.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Ownership of AI-Assisted Output
Work produced by an employee using AI tools as an assistant is owned by AIpportunity Pvt. Ltd. under the employment agreement, the same as any other work product.

5.2 Third-Party IP Risks
AI tools may generate content that resembles or replicates copyrighted material from training data. Employees must not use AI-generated creative content (text, images, code) in client deliverables without appropriate review for IP risk. Legal should be consulted for content in high-IP-risk domains.

6. REQUESTING NEW AI TOOL APPROVALS

Employees who wish to use an AI tool not on the approved list may submit an AI Tool Approval Request through the IT Self-Service portal. The request must specify: the tool name and vendor, the intended use case, the type of data that would be processed, and the business benefit. IT Security reviews requests within 10 working days and evaluates the tool's data handling practices, security certifications, and contract terms before approving or declining.

7. AI TOOLS USED IN HR AND PEOPLE DECISIONS

AI tools must not be used as the primary basis for HR decisions including hiring, performance assessment, salary decisions, or disciplinary actions. AI may assist in analysis but all such decisions require human judgement and review by HR. Any use of AI in HR processes must be disclosed to the HR Business Partner.

8. CONSEQUENCES OF POLICY VIOLATION

Unauthorised input of confidential data into consumer AI tools is treated as a data security incident and investigated accordingly. Depending on the severity, consequences range from mandatory re-training to disciplinary action. Repeated violations result in revocation of access to approved AI tools.

9. POLICY EVOLUTION

AI technology is evolving rapidly. This policy is reviewed quarterly and updated as the tool landscape changes. Employees are encouraged to report useful AI tools for consideration on the approved list via the IT Self-Service portal.

For queries or to report a potential AI data exposure, contact: security@aiopportunity.in
